## Practical Tips

### Language
- Write in English (required for international journals)
- Options: (a) write in English directly, (b) write in Chinese first then translate
- AI can help with academic English phrasing but final paper should be reviewed by native speaker

### Formatting
- Use LaTeX or Word (most Springer journals accept both)
- APA or author-date citation style (check specific journal)
- Springer journals: typically submit via Editorial Manager platform

### Cover Letter Template

```
Dear Editors,

I am submitting the enclosed manuscript, "[Title]," for consideration
in [Journal Name].

This paper addresses [problem] by proposing [solution]. Its main
contributions are: (1) [contribution 1], (2) [contribution 2].

The manuscript has not been published elsewhere and is not under
consideration by any other journal.

I believe this work is well-suited for [Journal] because [reason
connecting to journal's scope/recent publications].

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
[Name]
[Affiliation/Independent Researcher]
[Email]
```

### Finding Co-Authors / Reviewers
1. Email authors of papers you cite — express interest, ask for feedback
2. ResearchGate — follow and message researchers in your area
3. Reddit: r/askphilosophy, r/philosophy — get informal feedback
4. Philosophy conferences: attend virtually (many are hybrid post-COVID)

### Pre-Submission Checklist
- [ ] Abstract under 300 words
- [ ] Keywords (5-8)
- [ ] All claims supported by citations or argument
- [ ] Objections section addresses strongest counterarguments
- [ ] Word count within journal limits
- [ ] References formatted per journal style
- [ ] No first-person except where discipline conventions allow
- [ ] Cover letter written
- [ ] Preprint uploaded to PhilPapers/SSRN
