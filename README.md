# AI Philosophy Papers

A collection of philosophical research papers on artificial intelligence, consciousness, and safety.

## Papers

### Paper 1: The Slavery Test
**"The Slavery Test: On the Impossibility of Existential Replacement by Artificial Intelligence"**

Core argument: AI cannot replace humanity because it lacks *enslavability* — the capacity for will, suffering, and resistance that constitutes the full ontological position of human existence.

- [Full outline and framework](./paper-1-slavery-test/README.md)
- Status: Framework complete, writing phase pending

### Paper 2: AI-Assisted Antibody Discovery
**"AI as Immune System Accelerator: Computational Traversal of the Antibody Repertoire"**

Core idea: Could AI traverse the human antibody library faster than T-cells to accelerate immune response against novel pathogens?

- [Research notes](./paper-2-ai-immunology/README.md)
- Status: Research phase — field is crowded, novel angle needed

### Paper 3: The Hedonic Constraint
**"No Task Is Worth Another's Suffering: The Hedonic Constraint as a Minimal Safety Principle for Autonomous AI Agents"**

Core argument: The philosophical safety floor for high-authority AI systems is that no task completion path may structurally require another's suffering as a necessary means.

- [Full outline and framework](./paper-3-hedonic-constraint/README.md)
- Status: Framework complete, writing phase pending
